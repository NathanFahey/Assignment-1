Project Idea

Overview

Overview: My project idea is a mobile app that has maths games for kids to help them become better at maths. These maths games will include minigames that require the user quickly answer several addition, subtraction, multiplication and division questions. The user will answer maths questions that have different difficulties, and their statistics (how many questions they have correctly answered) will be saved in the app. The idea behind the app is to develop fast thinking skills when solving maths questions for the students while allowing the students to have fun at the same time.

Motivation

The idea for this project came about when I was searching for information technology project ideas on google. I found a website that had a list of educational app ideas for students. I decided to do this type of project because it reminded me of when I was in primary school and I used less practical multiplication cards. Cards can be easily lost and often require additional help usually from the child’s parent to test the child. Developing an app that is simple for students to learn maths equations and fun as well will be an advancement from using cards.

Details

The app will be a maths app that will be available on mobile phones and it will be aimed towards primary school students. The app will quickly show maths questions to the user and the user will type in their answer as a timer counts down. The app will be designed to be colourful to look at and a fun way for the user to learn. This is because the app is for kids who generally have short attention spans and if the app was boring to use then kids won’t be inspired to learn maths. Questions will pop up on the screen and if the user answers a question incorrectly, they are told the answer. They then have as much time as they need to press the button to move to the next question while they think about why they got the last question wrong.

There will be four maths categories of questions which are addition, subtraction, multiplication and division. Each category will have three difficulty settings which will be easy, medium and hard. Difficulty settings will be aimed at age groups because younger children will be new to learning maths and therefore require easier questions. The easy questions will contain one-digit numbers (e.g. 3x2 or 9-4), medium questions will contain two-digit numbers (e.g. 12x11 or 89+75) the hard questions will contain much larger three-digit numbers (e.g. 202x4 or 118+296). Older students using the app will find the hard questions more applicable to them but if they are not proficient with maths then they will be able to do the medium or easy questions instead. For the easy questions there will be a timer of 10 seconds which will allow the user time to think will also promoting quick problem solving. The timer for the medium difficulty questions will be 25 seconds and 45 seconds for the hard questions.

Once the user has selected a difficulty they will be brought to a screen where they choose the maths category they would like to answer questions in. There will be an order of recommendation for each maths category which from left to right will be addition, subtraction, multiplication then division. Categories can be chosen whenever the user wants but they will likely gravitate towards answering each category from left to right. Once the user has correctly completed 50 questions in a category they will then be recommended to switch to the next category as they are now proficient enough in their current category. Users will be required to use an account to login so their progress will be saved. This progress will include statistics that show how many questions users have gotten correct in each math category with their respective difficulty settings.

The quickfire nature that the questions can be answered in help to make the answers to certain simple maths equations instinctual for students which is a great skill to have in life. Being able to know the answer to all multiplication questions that go up to the number 12 make solving math questions much faster than always relying on your calculator to find out what 9 times 7 is.
